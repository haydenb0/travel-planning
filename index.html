The Destination

Why Loreto, Why Now

Warm, clear late-summer water and a marine park that begins just minutes from the marina.

Loreto fronts the Sea of Cortez — the body of water Jacques Cousteau called "the aquarium of the world." Its national marine park protects more than 2,000 km², five islands and over 800 species of fish, with 30-plus dive sites a short boat ride offshore. Late summer brings the warmest water and the season's best visibility, and your dates land just as the sea lions return to the colonies after their summer mating closure.

The Place

About Loreto

The oldest town in the Californias — and a quiet basecamp for far more than diving.

Loreto is the oldest European settlement on the Baja peninsula, founded on October 25, 1697 by the Jesuit priest Juan María de Salvatierra. Its mission — Nuestra Señora de Loreto — became the "head and mother" of the entire California mission chain, and El Camino Real, the royal road that eventually reached San Francisco, began at its door.

For roughly 80 years Loreto was the capital of the Californias (until the seat moved to Monterey in 1777, and remained Baja's capital until an 1829 hurricane). Today it's a designated Pueblo Mágico fronting the UNESCO-listed Gulf of California, with a cobblestoned historic core still gathered around the restored 1697 mission and its small Museo de las Misiones.

Beyond the dives

🐋 Whale watching

Dec–Mar the Sea of Cortez fills with blue, humpback & fin whales; day trips also reach the gray-whale lagoons of Magdalena Bay on the Pacific side.

⛪ Misión San Javier

A ~1-hour drive up into the Sierra de la Giganta to the 1699 Jesuit mission — the best-preserved baroque church in Baja, set in a tiny mountain oasis village.

🎣 Sportfishing

Year-round for snapper, grouper & yellowtail; summer brings migratory marlin, dorado and roosterfish. Half- and full-day panga charters from the marina.

🛶 Sea kayaking & islands

Paddle the island shores of the UNESCO-listed Gulf, or take a snorkel/beach boat tour to Coronado and Danzante on a non-dive day.

🏜️ Sierra de la Giganta

Desert canyons and ancient rock art rise right behind town — short hikes like Tabor Canyon, plus dramatic mountain drives and stargazing.

🚶 Malecón & Salvatierra

A palm-lined seaside promenade for sunrise walks, and the pedestrian Calle Salvatierra of boutiques and galleries leading up to the plaza and mission.

Underwater

The Dive Sites

Isla Coronado — "The Classic"

Level: Beginner–AdvancedDepth: 30–100 ftTrip: 4–5 hrs · 2 divesYour day: Sun Aug 30
▾

The closest island — an ancient dormant volcano and home to the resident sea-lion rookery. Dramatic lava formations create caverns, ledges and deep, steep walls ribbed with soft coral, over a bright white sandy bottom. Several spots also run as gentle drift dives.

Easy · all levels
🦭 Playful sea lions🪨 Lava caverns & ledges🧱 Steep soft-coral walls🐟 Schooling jacks🌊 Drift options

Isla Danzante & the Pinnacles — "The Deep South"

Level: Easy north / Advanced southDepth: to 100+ ftTrip: 5–6 hrs · 2 divesYour day: Mon Aug 31 🥂
▾

Some of the most spectacular diving in the whole park. The north half of Danzante is pristine and easy — walls, boulders, rocky slopes and sand mingle for huge biodiversity (moray eels, turtles, reef fish, rays). The southern tip and offshore seamounts are advanced: strong currents, deep steep pinnacles and walls thick with black coral, sea fans and gorgonians, with nudibranchs in great numbers and eagle rays and horn sharks cruising the deep.

Close to shore lies the wreck of the C-54 "Agustín Melgar," a 180-ft minesweeper sunk for diving — easy penetrations plus advanced training opportunities.

Advanced on the seamounts
🪸 Black-coral forests🦅 Eagle rays🦈 Horn sharks🐢 Turtles & morays🚢 C-54 wreck🐌 Nudibranchs

Isla del Carmen — "The Wild North"

Level: Intermediate–AdvancedDepth: variable, steepTrip: 4–5 hrs · 2 divesYour day: Tue Sep 1
▾

The park's largest island, and its most exposed coast. The steep north shore can get rougher when the wind blows — and those conditions are exactly what draw pelagics and big fish: mobula rays, great schools of barracuda, huge groupers and snappers, and baitballs. Underwater scenery is steep and abrupt, with deep drop-offs of giant boulders, tunnels and canyons. The boat ride passes towering limestone cliffs, sea caves and hidden beaches.

Intermediate–Advanced
🐡 Mobula rays🐟 Barracuda schools🪨 Tunnels & canyons🎣 Baitballs🦞 Groupers & snappers

Isla Montserrat & Las Galeras — "The Remote Splurge"

Level: AdvancedTrip: 7–8 hrs private · 2 divesOptional upgrade
▾

A full-day private charter (about 1 hr 40 min each way) to the park's most remote, least-visited island. You'll dive untouched seamounts like Bajo Sandia and La Reinita, sail past Las Galeras — twin islets in turquoise water with their own sea-lion colony — and watch for dolphins along the way, with a stop at a sandy cove on the way home. Booked as a private day (up to 6 divers).

A wonderful upgrade if you'd rather trade the Carmen day (or your buffer day) for something wilder and more exclusive — ask Dolphin Dive for the per-boat rate.

Advanced · private charter
🏝️ Remote seamounts🐬 Dolphins en route🦭 Las Galeras sea lions🐢 Olive ridley turtles🏖️ Beach stop

Coronado Night Dive — "Bioluminescence"

Trip: 4 hrs · sunset + 1 diveOptional
▾

Sail out at sunset with drinks, then dive after dark. The first half is spent in total darkness, waiting for the bioluminescence to peak — thousands of tiny planktonic lights swarming around you like floating through space. Then lights on to find lobsters, hermit crabs, grazing sea hares and hunting predators.

Note: a night dive adds nitrogen — only add this if you build an extra dive day, not the night before flying.

Getting There

Flights

One nonstop carrier from LAX — and it doesn't fly daily.

LAX → LTO nonstop · Alaska

Alaska is the only nonstop between LAX and Loreto. Roundtrip typically $350–600 pp in summer; flight time ~2 hrs. Book out Sat Aug 29 / back Thu Sep 3.

Alaska route page → · Compare on Google Flights →

⚠ Schedule note

The route is not daily — for your window it operates Thu, Sat & Sun, with late-morning/midday departures. That's why the trip runs Sat → Thu rather than other combinations. Re-confirm both flights operate that exact week when you book, as late-summer frequency can thin out.

Loreto's airport is tiny — a taxi or hotel shuttle into town is 5–10 minutes, and you won't need a rental car staying on the malecón.
