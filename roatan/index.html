The Destination

Why Roatán, Why Now

Warm water, 100+ dive sites five minutes offshore, and a village small enough to walk everywhere.

Roatán is the largest of Honduras's Bay Islands and sits along the Mesoamerican Reef — the second-largest barrier reef system on Earth. A wall runs close to shore for most of its north coast, so dramatic drop-offs, swim-through canyons, and two purpose-sunk wrecks are all a short boat ride from the dock. October puts you in the island's rainy season, but that trades only a little visibility for warm water, thinner crowds, and noticeably better off-season pricing on lodging.

Where On The Island

Regions of Roatán

The island splits into five distinct areas — here's how they compare before you narrow in on West End.

Roatán is 37 miles long and 5 miles wide, and its character changes a lot from west to east. Most visitors never get past the western third — West End, West Bay, and Sandy Bay — because that's where the diving infrastructure, restaurants, and walkable village life all live. Further east it gets progressively quieter, more residential, and less set up for a compact dive-focused week. Here's the full picture so you can see why West End keeps coming up as the base for this trip.

Sandy Bay and West Bay are both reasonable adjacent options (and factor into the Lodging comparison below); French Harbour/Coxen Hole and the East End are included here for a complete picture of the island, not as realistic bases for this itinerary.
The Island

About Roatán

Pirates, Cayman fishing families, and an English-speaking Caribbean culture — plus plenty to do topside.

Roatán was originally home to the Paya (Pech) people, later a Garifuna settlement point, and a notorious 17th-century pirate haven — Henry Morgan and others hid from Spanish patrols in its bays. English-speaking Cayman fishing families settled the island in the 1800s, which is why much of it, especially West End, still speaks a distinct Bay Islands English creole alongside Spanish. Roatán formally became part of Honduras in 1861 after a long dispute with Britain.

Beyond the dives

🐒 Gumbalimba Park

Nature preserve between West End and West Bay — capuchin monkeys, botanical gardens, a pirate cave, and West Bay beach access.

🦥 Sloth & monkey sanctuaries

Mayan Eden Eco Park, Daniel Johnson's, and others — hold a sloth, meet parrots, walk a butterfly trail.

🐬 Dolphin encounter (RIMS)

Roatán Institute for Marine Sciences at Anthony's Key — wild-born dolphins in a natural lagoon, from a wade-in intro to open-water swims.

🌴 Zip-lining

Canopy tours through the island's hills — one of only two certified canopy tours in Honduras.

🏖️ West Bay Beach

A short walk or $3 water-taxi ride from West End — powder sand and calm, snorkel-friendly water for a non-dive day.

🍫 Roatán Chocolate Factory

Free factory tour upstairs, cafe downstairs — an easy stop in the village between dives.

Underwater

The Dive Sites

Wall, wreck, and night diving — all reachable from West End. Tap any group to expand.

Roatán's wall runs close to the north shore, so most day-to-day diving sits within a 5–15 minute boat ride of West End. Add two purpose-sunk wrecks in Sandy Bay, a famous bioluminescent night dive, and — if you're willing to go south — the volcanic canyon at Mary's Place.

🌡️ Oct water ~81–82°F👁️ Viz 40–60+ ft (rainy season)🐢 Turtles & morays year-round🦈 Occasional reef sharks✨ Bioluminescence on new-moon nights

West End's home reef — walls, canyons & swim-throughs

Level: All levelsBoat ride: 5–15 min10+ named sites
▾

The wall runs right along West End and Sandy Bay's shore, so most day-to-day diving is close to the dock. Named sites include West End Wall, Pablo's Place, Hole in the Wall (iconic canyons, swim-throughs, even a small cave), Dennis' Drop-off (wall), Mandy's Eel Garden, Blue Channel, Canyon Reef (a network of swim-throughs, some shallow enough for Open Water divers with good buoyancy), Zack's Patch, Green Pearl (wall + swim-throughs + a cleaning station), and Spooky Channel (a deep channel where the walls narrow to ~6 ft apart near the top).

All levels
🪸 Wall dives🕳️ Swim-throughs🐠 Reef fish🧹 Cleaning stations🐍 Moray eels

Sandy Bay wrecks — El Aguila & Odyssey

Level: AdvancedDepth: ~110–115 ft~15 min by boat from West End
▾

El Aguila: a 210-ft cargo ship sunk in 1997, broken into three sections by Hurricane Mitch (1998) — each piece explorable separately, resting around 100–110 ft, just a couple hundred yards offshore. Odyssey: the Caribbean's largest shipwreck dive — 300 ft long, 50 ft wide, 85 ft tall, sunk in 2002, resting at ~110–115 ft in Sandy Bay. Both need Advanced certification (or a guided deep-dive briefing) given the depth.

Advanced — depth & possible current
🚢 Two full wrecks🦈 Occasional reef sharks🐡 Groupers & snapper🐍 Green moray eels

Night diving — West End's "String of Pearls"

Level: All levelsLocal reefBest on new-moon nights
▾

A Firelight/bioluminescence night dive, best about an hour after sunset on a new-moon night: divers turn off all lights to watch tiny ostracod crustaceans glow — Roatán's famous "string of pearls" effect drifting upward like stars. Most West End shops offer this on set nights (Roatan Divers runs Tue/Thu); Splash Inn and Sun Divers also offer it à la carte.

All levels
✨ Bioluminescence🌑 Best on new moon🔦 Torch required👥 Min. divers varies by shop

Mary's Place (optional — south side)

Level: AdvancedDepth: ~100 ft canyonFar from West End
▾

A dramatic volcanic fissure/canyon off Brick Bay near French Harbour, widely called one of the best dives in the Caribbean — steep walls packed with coral and sponges forming a narrow labyrinth. Too far for most West End shops to run as a standard day; Splash Inn's "South Side Special 2" (Mary's Place + Prince Albert wreck, min. 4 divers, $50 with a package/$135 à la carte) is the most affordable route from a West-End base, and CoCo View (Category 1) sits close enough to include it more easily.

Advanced · special trip required
🌋 Volcanic canyon🪸 Dense coral & sponges🐍 Resident green moray📅 Weather & min.-diver dependent
Decision needed: is Mary's Place worth a special day, or do you stay reef/wreck-focused and skip it? Splash Inn's combo trip makes it cheap to say yes without leaving West End.

After the Dives

Where to Eat — West End

Over 40 restaurants and street vendors along a single walkable strip — here's the complete, top-reviewed shortlist by category.

West End's dining scene is the most diverse on the island — Honduran street food, Texas-style BBQ, Italian, Thai, healthy cafes, and beachfront bars all sit within a mile-long strip. This list pulls from local rankings and reviews and groups everything by what you're in the mood for, rather than one flat list.

🥇 The must-eats

Sandy Buns Bakery & Cafe

The runaway #1 in West End. Texan-run bakery-cafe — legendary cinnamon rolls, smoked brisket, Dirty Bird chicken sandwich. Breakfast/lunch only, closed Sundays; go early, only 10–12 tables.

Roatán Oasis

Hillside fine dining, winner of Honduras' Best Restaurant (World Culinary Awards). Internationally inspired menu, craft cocktails. Needs transport + reservations — the special-occasion pick.

Sundowners Beach Bar

West End's iconic sunset spot right on Half Moon Bay — burgers, wraps, tropical cocktails (try the Monkey La La), live music most evenings.

🍝 Italian & pizza

Pazzo's

Six tables, homemade pasta, BYOB. Reservation essential — the Beef Carpaccio is the standout starter.

Drunken Sailor

Northern-Italian owner, homemade pasta and some of the best pizza and gnocchi on the island. The lasagna is a favorite.

Junior's Patio

Fresh dough daily, wood-fired, build-your-own pie in a string-lit courtyard. BYOB, cash only, limited hours (Mon–Fri, ~4:30–8pm) — call ahead.

Bigros / C Level Pizzeria

4.8 stars, Travelers' Choice award. Strong gluten-free and vegan-cheese options.

🐟 Seafood & grills

Ginger's Caribbean Grill

Half Moon Bay alcove, sustainably-sourced fish from local fishermen. Lionfish tacos and bang bang shrimp are the draw.

Argentinian Grill

Open-flame grilled steaks, pork, chicken, shrimp and lobster. Beachfront seating between the palms overlooking Half Moon Bay.

Creole's Rotisserie Chicken

The island's go-to rotisserie chicken — $20 for a whole bird + 4 sides. Limited nightly supply; arrive by 7pm.

Anthony's Chicken

Creole's jerk-chicken rival — spicier, and reliably open later in the evening.

🌮 Local & cheap eats

Yahongreh

The island's best baleada for about $2 — cash only, breakfast/lunch, tucked into the Chili's hotel grounds.

Calelu's

Another top pick for traditional Honduran baleadas.

Loretta's Island Cooking

Home-style island cooking in Loretta's own yard in Half Moon Bay — fried chicken, coconut rice, and an over-the-top lobster pasta. A local hidden gem.

☕ Cafes & healthy options

Café Escondido

Upper-deck cafe above West End Divers — smoothies, healthy breakfast/lunch, good wifi for a slow morning.

Crisp

Poke bowls, cold-pressed juices, salads — vegan/vegetarian/gluten-free friendly, at Coconut Tree Plaza.

Café de Palo

Serene jungle-garden cafe inside the Arco Orquídeas hotel at the end of Half Moon Bay. Breakfast/lunch only, closed Tuesdays.

Roatán Chocolate Factory

Free factory tour upstairs, cafe downstairs — an easy between-dives stop.

🍽️ Worth knowing about

Stowaway Gastrobar

Newest arrival, Mediterranean-inspired, daily-changing menu cooked to order. A little off the main strip north of Half Moon Bay.

Thai Blue Elephant

Authentic Thai for 16+ years, Travelers' Choice award — pad Thai, yellow curry, tom yum. Higher prices ($7–44) but worth it.

Splash Inn Restaurant

Open to the public, not just hotel guests — solid international menu on a terrace over Half Moon Bay (see the Lodging section for its independent rating).

Pura Vida

Newer Costa Rican spot on Half Moon Bay next to Ginger's — try the Rondon soup and tropical sangria.

Lily Pond House

Transforms into the island's most romantic dinner spot after dark — a good anniversary-style pick if the trip calls for one.

Mila del Mar (The Beach House)

Two stories above Half Moon Bay — arguably the best sunset view of any restaurant in West End, plus a weekend dock happy hour.

Practical notes: cash is still preferred at several spots (Junior's is cash-only; Sandy Buns and Sundowners also take Venmo). Tipping runs 10–15%, similar to the US. Most places are walk-in, but Pazzo's and Junior's Patio genuinely need a reservation/advance call. If you want to range further, West Bay is a $3–5 water-taxi or short taxi ride away and has its own scene (Beachers, JavaVine, Bananarama's Thirsty Turtle).

💵 Pricing & arrival

Booking with airline miles, not cash — plan on ~$75–150 pp in taxes/fees (~$225–450 for the group) rather than the ~$800 pp cash fares seen in searches. Saver-level award space for 3 seats together can be harder to find than the cash fare — book as early as your program allows and stay flexible on connection city.
